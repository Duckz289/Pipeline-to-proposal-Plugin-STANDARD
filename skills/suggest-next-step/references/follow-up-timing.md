# Follow-Up Timing Guide

## Email Timing

| Situation | Recommended timing |
|---|---|
| Cold first touch, no response | Follow up after 3-5 business days. |
| Follow-up #1, no response | Follow up again after 5-7 days. |
| Proposal sent | Follow up after 2-3 business days. |
| Post-demo | Send recap within 2 hours, then follow up after 3-5 days. |
| Re-engagement | Wait at least 30 days unless there is a new trigger. |

## Tracking-Adjusted Timing

Use only when tracking data is explicitly provided:

| Signal | Timing adjustment |
|---|---|
| Proposal viewed multiple times in 24 hours | Follow up the same or next business day with a specific question. |
| Pricing or implementation section viewed/clicked | Ask whether they want to review scope, pricing, or rollout assumptions. |
| Email opened but no click/reply | Keep the follow-up light; do not imply high intent. |
| Link clicked but no reply | Follow up around the clicked resource or related pain. |
| No tracking data | Use default timing and say no open/click/view signal is available. |

## Channel Mix

Cold outreach:

```text
Day 0: Email
Day 4: Follow-up email
Day 7: LinkedIn or call attempt
Day 10: Break-up email
Day 30+: Re-engage if there is a new trigger
```

Post-call:

```text
Same day: Recap and promised resources
Day 2: Proposal/resources if promised
Day 5: Follow-up with specific question
Day 10: Final nudge or stakeholder escalation
```

Post-proposal:

```text
Day 0: Send proposal
Day 3: Ask about the most relevant section or decision criteria
Day 7: Offer to walk through it
Day 12: Confirm whether to pause or adjust scope
```

## Urgency Triggers

Reach out sooner when:

- Prospect announces funding, expansion, or leadership change.
- Prospect posts about a problem you solve.
- Proposal opened several times if tracking exists.
- Mutual introduction happens.
- Budget cycle or event deadline is near.

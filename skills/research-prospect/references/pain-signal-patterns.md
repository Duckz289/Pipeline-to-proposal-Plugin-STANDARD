# Pain Signal Patterns

Use these patterns to infer possible pain points. Label them as inferred unless directly supported
by CRM notes, prospect words, or credible public sources.

## SaaS / Technology

| Signal | Likely interpretation |
|---|---|
| Hiring several SDRs/AEs | Scaling outbound and needing repeatable prep. |
| RevOps role posted | Sales process and data quality are becoming important. |
| Recent funding | Pressure to grow pipeline quickly. |
| New market/product launch | Need new messaging and proposal assets. |
| Many tools in sales job posts | Process may be complex or fragmented. |

## Professional Services

| Signal | Likely interpretation |
|---|---|
| Hiring business development | Moving beyond referral-led growth. |
| Few case studies on website | Sales proof may be weak or unstructured. |
| Founder-led sales | Founder time bottleneck. |
| Expansion to new market | Need new prospecting and proposal process. |

## Manufacturing / Distribution

| Signal | Likely interpretation |
|---|---|
| Adding inside sales team | Moving from relationship/field sales to hybrid sales. |
| ERP/CRM modernization | Open to operational tooling. |
| Dealer/distributor network | Multi-stakeholder sales complexity. |
| Long quote/proposal cycles | Proposal automation may create time savings. |

## Agencies

| Signal | Likely interpretation |
|---|---|
| Many custom proposals | Proposal quality and speed matter. |
| Founder/partner selling | Senior time bottleneck. |
| New niche/service line | Need repeatable outbound messaging. |

## Universal Signals

- "Manual", "spreadsheet", "CRM hygiene", "pipeline visibility" in jobs or posts.
- Long response time complaints.
- Fast sales hiring.
- New VP Sales, CRO, Head of Revenue, or RevOps hire.
- Repeated follow-up or no-response pattern in CRM.
- Proposal sent but no meeting booked.

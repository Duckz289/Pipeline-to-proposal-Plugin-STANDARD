# Ideal Customer Profile Definitions

Use these profiles to score and qualify prospects.

## Tier 1: Perfect Fit

- B2B company with 10-200 employees.
- Has a sales team of 5+ reps.
- Uses a CRM such as HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, or Zoho.
- Sales cycle is longer than 14 days.
- Average contract value is likely above $5,000.
- There is visible outbound, RevOps, sales enablement, or proposal work.

Signals:

- Hiring SDRs, AEs, RevOps, Sales Ops, or Sales Enablement.
- Leadership talks about pipeline, conversion, revenue efficiency, or expansion.
- Sales team is scaling after funding or new market launch.

## Tier 2: Good Fit

- B2B company with 5-10 or 200-500 employees.
- Sales process exists but may be founder-led or inconsistent.
- CRM usage is unclear.
- Some outbound or proposal activity is visible.
- The team may need standardization before deeper automation.

## Tier 3: Weak Fit

- Mostly B2C or transactional sales.
- Fewer than 5 employees.
- No sign of repeatable sales process.
- Deal size is likely too small.
- Relationship is mostly self-serve or marketplace-driven.

## Disqualifiers

- Pure e-commerce or marketplace with no B2B sales motion.
- Government/NGO if the seller does not support public procurement.
- Direct competitor.
- No clear economic buyer.

## Scoring

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 8-10 | Prioritize. Strong ICP and clear trigger. |
| 5-7 | Worth research. Needs validation. |
| 1-4 | Low priority unless user has special context. |
